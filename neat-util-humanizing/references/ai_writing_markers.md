# AI Writing Markers

Comprehensive catalog of 33 AI writing markers adapted from humanizer skill + our discoveries.

**Usage:** Reference this when applying [rule_cluster_detection.md](rule_cluster_detection.md) to identify specific markers in text.

---

## Content Markers (8)

**1. Inflated Significance** - Treating mundane things as profound:
- "testament to", "embodiment of", "speaks to the power of", "underscores the importance of"

**2. Promotional Language** - Marketing-speak in non-marketing contexts:
- "world-class", "cutting-edge", "innovative", "game-changing", "revolutionary"

**3. Vague Attributions** - Claims without specific sources:
- "studies show", "experts say", "research indicates", "it's been proven that"

**4. Generic Superlatives** - Embellishment words:
- "exceptional", "outstanding", "proven", "remarkable", "extraordinary"

**5. Abstract Elevation** - Making concrete things abstract:
- "journey" (for process), "landscape" (for situation/field), "tapestry" (for combination), "ecosystem" (for set of things)

**6. Formulaic Conclusions** - Predictable endings:
- "In conclusion...", "Ultimately...", "At the end of the day...", "The bottom line is..."

**7. False Precision** - Overly specific without basis:
- Exact percentages without data, arbitrary timeframes, unfounded projections

**8. Universal Claims** - Absolutes that can't be true:
- "everyone agrees", "always"/"never" (without qualification), "the only way"

## Language/Grammar Markers (13)

**9. AI Vocabulary** - High-frequency AI words:
- testament, vibrant, nestled, delve, tapestry, landscape (abstract), robust, seamless

**10. Copula Phrases** - "To be" avoidance:
- "serves as", "stands as", "acts as", "functions as"

**11. "Not just X but Y" Construction**:
- "not just a tool but a revolution", "not only X but also Y"

**12. Passive Voice Overuse**:
- "it was determined that", "it can be seen that", "it is believed that"

**13. Over-Qualification** - Excessive intensifiers:
- "very", "highly", "extremely", "strongly", "significantly"

**14. Hedge Words Clusters**:
- "quite possibly", "perhaps maybe", "somewhat relatively"

**15. Academic Throat-Clearing**:
- "It is important to note that", "It should be emphasized that", "It is worth mentioning that"

**16. Nominalization Overload** - Verbs turned to nouns:
- "implementation" (vs "implement"), "utilization" (vs "use"), "facilitation" (vs "facilitate")

**17. Synonym Stacking**:
- "fast and quick", "complete and total", "each and every"

**18. Jargon Without Context** - Technical terms unexplained in non-technical writing

**19. Abstract Before Concrete** - Leading with theory instead of examples

**20. Passive Constructions** - Removing human agency:
- "mistakes were made", "it was decided"

**21. Modal Verb Overuse** - Excessive "could", "would", "should", "might"

## Style Markers (7)

**22. Em-Dash Saturation** - Multiple em-dashes in single paragraph:
- Context-dependent: formal docs = zero, blogs = moderate allowed
- High-confidence tell when 3+ in same section

**23. Title Case Headers in Body** - Capitalizing Like This in Mid-Sentence

**24. Rule of Three** - Three parallel examples/sections:
- "fast, efficient, and reliable", "Plan, Execute, Deliver", three bullet points per section

**25. Negative Parallelisms** - "Not X, not Y, but Z" constructions

**26. Outline-Like Sections** - Numbered lists everywhere:
- "First...", "Second...", "Finally..."

**27. Emoji Injection** - Emojis in professional/formal text where inappropriate

**28. Curly Quotes** - Typographer's quotes (") vs straight quotes (") - ChatGPT signal

---

## Communication Markers (5)

**29. Collaborative Artifacts** - Unnecessary "we" language:
- "let's dive in", "let's explore", "we'll discover"

**30. Sycophantic Tone** - Overly helpful AI voice:
- "I'd be happy to", "I hope this helps", "Feel free to"

**31. Diff-Anchored Writing** - Comparing to previous versions:
- "as mentioned earlier", "building on what we discussed", "in contrast to the previous approach"

**32. Meta-Commentary** - Explaining what you're about to do:
- "In this section, we will...", "The following will demonstrate..."

**33. Transition Overload** - Excessive connective tissue:
- "Furthermore...", "Moreover...", "Additionally...", "In addition..."

## Our Additions (From Practice)

**Corporate Embellishment Markers:**
- "demonstrated consistent [ROLE] capabilities"
- "proven ability to"
- "strongly recommended"
- "exceeding target/expectations"
- "exceptional [NOUN] through"

**Technical Writing Anti-Markers:**
- "simply" (implying ease)
- "just" (minimizing complexity)
- "easily" (assuming reader level)
- "obviously" (condescending)

**Email-Specific Markers:**
- "I hope this email finds you well"
- "Please don't hesitate to"
- "At your earliest convenience"
- "Per our conversation"
