# Rule: Preserve Human Authenticity Markers

## Principle

Preserve contradictions, mixed feelings, rough edges, and idiosyncrasies when refining writing - they signal human authenticity.

## Why This Rule Exists

Over-polishing removes authenticity. Human writing has rough edges - mixed feelings ("unsure if I loved it or just liked the idea"), contradictions, tangents, specific friction points. AI-generated text is uniformly smooth and resolved. Removing these "imperfections" makes writing sound MORE AI-generated, not less.

## How to Apply

### Preserve (Don't Smooth Over)

**Mixed Feelings**
- "I wasn't sure if I loved it"
- "It was good, but I can't quite explain why"
- "I'm still figuring this out"

**Contradictions**
- "It was frustrating but rewarding"
- "The process was messy yet effective"
- "I loved it and hated it at the same time"

**Specific Friction**
- "six-story staircase with no elevator"
- "three failed attempts before it worked"
- "spent two hours debugging a typo"

**Tangents**
- Brief off-topic observations that add personality
- Side comments that reveal thought process
- Unexpected connections

**Unresolved Thoughts**
- "This might not work for everyone"
- "I'm not entirely convinced yet"
- "Still working through the implications"

**Honest Uncertainty**
- "I could be wrong about this"
- "Not sure if this is the best approach"
- "Depends on your situation"

---

### Remove (AI Smoothness)

**Generic Positivity**
- "unforgettable experience"
- "absolutely perfect"
- "couldn't have been better"

**Resolved Conclusions**
- "ultimately it was the right choice"
- "in the end, everything worked out perfectly"
- "proved to be exactly what we needed"

**Abstract Elevation**
- "journey of discovery"
- "transformative experience"  
- "testament to resilience"

**Diplomatic Hedging**
- "somewhat challenging in certain respects"
- "relatively straightforward in most cases"
- "generally speaking, quite effective"

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Blog Post

❌ **Over-polished (loses authenticity):**
> "The hiking experience was exceptional, offering breathtaking views and unforgettable moments throughout the journey."

✅ **Preserved authenticity:**
> "The hike had incredible views, but the six-story staircase climb with no elevator? I'm still not sure if I loved it or just liked the idea of loving it."

**What was preserved:** Mixed feelings, specific friction, honest uncertainty

---

### Example 2: Technical Documentation

❌ **Over-polished:**
> "This approach provides a seamless solution that perfectly addresses all edge cases."

✅ **Preserved authenticity:**
> "This approach handles most cases. Edge case with nested arrays fails - workaround in issue #47."

**What was preserved:** Honest limitation, specific unsolved problem

---

## When NOT to Preserve

Don't preserve if:
- **Errors/typos** - Fix actual mistakes
- **Unclear writing** - Clarify genuinely confusing passages  
- **Inappropriate tone** - Remove unprofessional content if context demands
- **Factual errors** - Correct wrong information
- **Offensive content** - Remove if inappropriate for audience

Preserve rough edges ≠ preserve mistakes.

---

## Application by Context

| Context | What to Preserve | What to Remove |
|---------|------------------|----------------|
| **Blog** | Mixed feelings, tangents, first-person uncertainty | Generic positivity, formulaic conclusions |
| **Corporate** | Specific challenges faced, honest limitations | Embellishment, exaggeration |
| **Technical** | Known bugs, workarounds, actual constraints | Marketing language, false certainty |
| **Email** | Partial agreement, honest questions | Sycophantic tone, over-apology |
